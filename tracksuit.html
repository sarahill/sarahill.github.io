Design and Front-end Development

Making strides to a Design System

When Tracker first hit the scene 10 years ago, it was seen as one of the leaders amongst product management tools. Over time, more and more tools that solve similar problems in varying ways have started to pop up. Recently, there seems to be a new product management app popping up weekly.

The team started wondering "How can we build quicker?". As the team started to tackle bigger themes that required more extensive design and development work communication and consistency started to break down. As a result, we introduced the Pivotal Tracker Styleguide.

Over the past year the Styleguide has evolved from a living guide made up of html and css based components to a pattern library with both react and elm components from the main app's codebase. To continue to build out the library, the team has chores in their development backlogs to continue to add to and update the pattern library.

Designing with components

To continue to bridge the gap between development and design, we created a Sketch UI Kit that is composed of Sketch symbols built to mirror the components found in the Pattern Library. Having these sets of tools starts to bring us closer to speaking the same language and thinking about the pieces of the UI in a similar way.

What's next

The Tracker Design and Development teams are continuing to refine the process of maintaing and scaling the Design System. As we continue to tackle some bigger user problems, we continue to think about the best user experience and the best way to capture that in the code.
